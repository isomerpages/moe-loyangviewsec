<h2><strong>1. Purpose and Introduction</strong></h2>
<p>This Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) establishes guidelines for the responsible
and effective use of Personal Learning Devices (PLDs) and educational technology
resources at Loyang View Secondary School. Aligned with our purpose of
empowering every individual to SHINE, this policy creates a safe, productive
digital learning environment that enhances educational outcomes whilst
protecting the privacy, safety, and wellbeing of all members of our school
community.</p>
<p>As we nurture our students to become lifelong learners, future-ready leaders,
and active citizens, technology serves as a powerful tool that supports
21st-century learning when used appropriately. This policy ensures that
our educational technology resources are used in ways that promote learning,
foster digital citizenship, and maintain a respectful school environment
where our values of Integrity, Respect, Responsibility, Resilience, and
Compassion guide all digital interactions.</p>
<p>Through responsible use of technology, we cultivate our school culture
of Learn, Value, Shine - where students learn effectively through digital
tools, value respectful online behaviour, and shine as responsible digital
citizens in our vibrant and caring school community.</p>
<h2><strong>2. Scope and Application</strong></h2>
<p>This policy applies to:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>All students, teachers, staff, and visitors using school technology resources</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Personal Learning Devices (iPads) provided by or used within the school</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>All educational technology platforms and applications accessible through
school systems</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Use of school technology resources both on and off campus during school-related
activities</p>
</li>
</ul>
<h2><strong>3. Technology Resources Covered</strong></h2>
<p>Our educational technology ecosystem includes:</p>
<p><strong>Personal Learning Devices:</strong> School-issued iPads managed
through school-administered Apple IDs with access to 200GB complimentary
iCloud storage for educational backup purposes.</p>
<p><strong>Learning Platforms:</strong> Google Classroom, Student Learning
Space (SLS), Microsoft 365, and other curriculum-supporting applications
approved through our JAMF Student app management system.</p>
<p><strong>Interactive Learning Tools:</strong> Educational applications including
Kahoot, Padlet, ClassPoint, and other teacher-approved interactive platforms
that enhance classroom engagement and learning.</p>
<p><strong>School Management Systems:</strong> EduPage, Wayground, and other
administrative platforms that support school operations and communication.</p>
<h2><strong>4. Core Requirements and Technical Standards</strong></h2>
<h3><strong>4.1 Device Management Application (DMA) Requirement</strong></h3>
<p><strong>All Personal Learning Devices must have the Device Management Application (DMA) installed and active before they can be used in school for lessons.</strong> This
requirement is non-negotiable and ensures:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Appropriate content filtering and security measures</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Compliance with school technology policies</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Protection of student data and privacy</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Effective classroom management of digital resources</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Devices without DMA will be treated according to existing school smartphone
policies, including immediate confiscation and storage in the Student Welfare
room. Further consequences may apply, up to and including withholding devices
until parents collect them personally.</p>
<h3><strong>4.2 Apple ID and Account Management</strong></h3>
<p>Students must use only school-managed Apple IDs provided by Loyang View
Secondary School. These accounts provide:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Access to 200GB complimentary iCloud storage for educational backup purposes</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Secure access to approved educational applications</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Proper data protection and privacy controls</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Integration with school learning management systems</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Personal Apple IDs are not permitted on school devices to ensure security,
appropriate content access, and compliance with educational data protection
requirements.</p>
<h3><strong>4.3 Application Installation and Management</strong></h3>
<p>All applications must be installed through the JAMF Student app using
the school's approved whitelist. Students may only:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Download applications that appear in their JAMF Student app catalogue</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Use applications that have been vetted and approved by the school</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Access educational tools that support curriculum objectives</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Attempting to bypass the JAMF system, install unauthorised applications,
or modify device settings is strictly prohibited and will result in disciplinary
action.</p>
<h3><strong>4.4 Camera and Recording Policy</strong></h3>
<p>The camera and recording functions on PLDs may only be used with explicit
approval from the subject teacher. This policy protects:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Student and staff privacy rights</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Confidential classroom discussions and activities</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Compliance with data protection regulations</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>A safe and comfortable learning environment for all</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Unauthorised photography, video recording, or audio recording in classrooms
or school premises is strictly prohibited and may result in serious disciplinary
consequences.</p>
<h2><strong>5. Acceptable Use Guidelines</strong></h2>
<h3><strong>5.1 Educational Use</strong></h3>
<p>Personal Learning Devices and educational technology resources should
be used to support learning and academic growth. Acceptable uses include:</p>
<p><strong>Academic Activities:</strong> Completing assignments, conducting
research for school projects, accessing curriculum materials, participating
in online lessons, and engaging with educational content that supports
classroom learning objectives.</p>
<p><strong>Collaborative Learning:</strong> Working with classmates on group
projects through approved platforms, participating in educational discussions,
sharing academic resources, and engaging in peer learning activities under
teacher supervision.</p>
<p><strong>Communication:</strong> Communicating with teachers and peers about
academic matters through approved school platforms such as Google Classroom,
SLS, or other designated channels for educational purposes.</p>
<p><strong>Creative and Critical Thinking:</strong> Using approved applications
to create presentations, digital portfolios, multimedia projects, and other
creative works that demonstrate learning and support curriculum objectives.</p>
<h3><strong>5.2 Responsible Digital Citizenship</strong></h3>
<p>As members of the Loyang View Secondary School community, students are
expected to demonstrate our school values in all digital interactions:</p>
<p><strong>Integrity:</strong> Being honest in digital communications, properly
citing sources, avoiding plagiarism, and representing oneself truthfully
in online academic environments.</p>
<p><strong>Respect:</strong> Treating others with courtesy and consideration
in digital spaces, respecting diverse perspectives, and maintaining appropriate
language and behaviour in all online interactions.</p>
<p><strong>Responsibility:</strong> Taking ownership of digital actions, protecting
personal and others' information, following school guidelines, and using
technology resources thoughtfully and purposefully.</p>
<p><strong>Resilience:</strong> Learning from digital mistakes, seeking help
when facing online challenges, and developing healthy technology habits
that support long-term wellbeing.</p>
<p><strong>Compassion:</strong> Supporting classmates in digital learning
environments, reporting concerning online behaviour to trusted adults,
and contributing positively to the school's online community.</p>
<h3><strong>5.3 Data and Privacy Protection</strong></h3>
<p>Students are expected to protect both their own and others' personal information
by maintaining confidentiality of login credentials, respecting privacy
settings on school platforms, avoiding sharing personal details in public
digital spaces, and reporting any suspected security breaches to teachers
or IT support immediately.</p>
<h2><strong>6. Prohibited Activities</strong></h2>
<p>The following activities are strictly forbidden when using Personal Learning
Devices and educational technology resources:</p>
<h3><strong>6.1 Inappropriate Content and Behaviour</strong></h3>
<p><strong>Accessing Inappropriate Material:</strong> Viewing, downloading,
or sharing content that is violent, sexually explicit, discriminatory,
illegal, or otherwise unsuitable for an educational environment.</p>
<p><strong>Cyberbullying and Harassment:</strong> Engaging in any form of
online bullying, harassment, intimidation, or threatening behaviour towards
students, staff, or any member of the school community through digital
platforms.</p>
<p><strong>Inappropriate Communication:</strong> Using offensive, discriminatory,
or disrespectful language in digital communications, including but not
limited to racist, sexist, or other prejudicial remarks.</p>
<h3><strong>6.2 Security and System Violations</strong></h3>
<p><strong>Bypassing Security Measures:</strong> Attempting to circumvent
content filters, firewalls, or other security systems put in place by the
school, including using VPNs or proxy servers to access blocked content.</p>
<p><strong>Unauthorised Access:</strong> Attempting to access other users'
accounts, files, or personal information, or trying to gain administrative
privileges on school devices or networks.</p>
<p><strong>System Tampering:</strong> Modifying device settings, attempting
to remove or disable the DMA, jailbreaking devices, or installing unauthorised
software outside the JAMF Student app system.</p>
<h3><strong>6.3 Misuse of School Resources</strong></h3>
<p><strong>Commercial Activities:</strong> Using school technology resources
for personal business, commercial transactions, or any profit-making activities
unrelated to education.</p>
<p><strong>Excessive Personal Use:</strong> Using devices for entertainment,
gaming, or social media during instructional time without explicit teacher
permission and educational purpose.</p>
<p><strong>Bandwidth Abuse:</strong> Streaming non-educational content, downloading
large files for personal use, or engaging in activities that consume excessive
network resources and impact others' learning.</p>
<h3><strong>6.4 Privacy and Legal Violations</strong></h3>
<p><strong>Unauthorised Recording:</strong> Taking photographs, videos, or
audio recordings without proper teacher approval and consent from all parties
involved.</p>
<p><strong>Copyright Infringement:</strong> Illegally downloading, sharing,
or distributing copyrighted materials including music, movies, software,
or other protected content.</p>
<p><strong>Identity Misrepresentation:</strong> Impersonating others online,
creating fake accounts, or misrepresenting one's identity in digital communications.</p>
<h2><strong>7. Consequences and Enforcement</strong></h2>
<h3><strong>7.1 Progressive Discipline Framework</strong></h3>
<p>Loyang View Secondary School employs a progressive discipline approach
that emphasises learning from mistakes while maintaining clear boundaries.
Consequences are determined based on the severity of the violation, frequency
of incidents, and the student's response to corrective guidance.</p>
<h3><strong>7.2 Consequence Levels</strong></h3>
<p><strong>Level 1 - Minor Violations</strong> First-time minor infractions
such as inappropriate use during class time or accessing non-educational
content may result in verbal warnings, temporary restriction of device
privileges during specific lessons, reflection activities focused on digital
citizenship, or additional supervision during technology use.</p>
<p><strong>Level 2 - Moderate Violations</strong> Repeated minor violations
or moderate infractions such as attempting to bypass security measures,
inappropriate online communication, or misuse of school platforms may result
in temporary suspension of PLD privileges for one to several days, parent
notification and conference, completion of digital citizenship education
modules, or written reflection on the impact of actions on the learning
community.</p>
<p><strong>Level 3 - Serious Violations</strong> Serious infractions such
as cyberbullying, accessing highly inappropriate content, unauthorised
recording, or attempting to compromise system security may result in extended
suspension of technology privileges, formal disciplinary action according
to school policies, mandatory counselling or intervention programmes, or
involvement of external authorities where appropriate.</p>
<h3><strong>7.3 Device Management Violations</strong></h3>
<p><strong>Non-Compliance with DMA Requirements:</strong> Devices without
proper DMA installation will be immediately confiscated and stored in the
Student Welfare room. Students will face progressive consequences that
may include device confiscation until parents collect personally, temporary
suspension from technology-enhanced lessons, and additional digital citizenship
education requirements.</p>
<h3><strong>7.4 Restoration and Learning Focus</strong></h3>
<p>All disciplinary actions emphasise restoration and learning rather than
purely punitive measures. Students will have opportunities to demonstrate
understanding of appropriate technology use, contribute positively to the
digital learning community, and rebuild trust through consistent responsible
behaviour.</p>
<h3><strong>7.5 Appeal Process</strong></h3>
<p>Students and parents may request a review of disciplinary decisions through
the school's standard appeal procedures, ensuring fair and transparent
handling of all technology-related incidents.</p>
<h2><strong>8. Monitoring and Privacy Considerations</strong></h2>
<h3><strong>8.1 Network and Device Monitoring</strong></h3>
<p>Loyang View Secondary School maintains appropriate monitoring systems
to ensure student safety, network security, and compliance with this Acceptable
Use Policy. The school reserves the right to monitor network traffic, review
device usage logs, and examine content accessed through school technology
resources when necessary for educational, safety, or disciplinary purposes.</p>
<h3><strong>8.2 Content Filtering and Safety Measures</strong></h3>
<p>All school devices and networks employ content filtering systems designed
to block inappropriate material and protect students from harmful online
content. These systems are regularly updated and maintained to provide
age-appropriate access to educational resources whilst preventing exposure
to unsuitable material.</p>
<h3><strong>8.3 Data Collection and Storage</strong></h3>
<p><strong>Educational Data:</strong> The school collects and stores student
work, assessment data, and learning analytics through approved educational
platforms to support academic progress and personalised learning. This
data is used solely for educational purposes and is protected according
to applicable data protection regulations.</p>
<p><strong>Usage Monitoring:</strong> Basic usage statistics and security
logs may be collected to ensure system performance, identify technical
issues, and maintain network security. This information is used for administrative
purposes and is not shared beyond authorised school personnel.</p>
<h3><strong>8.4 Privacy Protections</strong></h3>
<p><strong>Student Privacy Rights:</strong> Whilst monitoring is necessary
for safety and security, the school respects student privacy and conducts
monitoring activities proportionally and appropriately. Personal communications
and private work are only reviewed when there are legitimate concerns about
safety, security, or policy violations.</p>
<p><strong>Data Security:</strong> All student data collected through school
technology systems is stored securely, accessed only by authorised personnel,
and protected according to established data protection protocols.</p>
<h3><strong>8.5 Transparency and Communication</strong></h3>
<p>Students and parents will be informed about monitoring practices through
this policy and other school communications. The school maintains transparency
about what information is collected, how it is used, and who has access
to it, whilst balancing this openness with necessary security measures.</p>
<h2><strong>9. Digital Citizenship and Ethics</strong></h2>
<h3><strong>9.1 Foundations of Digital Citizenship</strong></h3>
<p>As students of Loyang View Secondary School, you are called to be exemplary
digital citizens who embody our school values in all online interactions.
Digital citizenship encompasses the responsible, ethical, and safe use
of technology to engage with the digital world as informed, respectful,
and contributing members of society.</p>
<h3><strong>9.2 Ethical Use of Information</strong></h3>
<p><strong>Academic Integrity:</strong> Students must demonstrate honesty
in all digital academic work by properly citing sources and references,
avoiding plagiarism and unauthorised collaboration, creating original work
that reflects personal learning and understanding, and respecting intellectual
property rights of content creators.</p>
<p><strong>Information Literacy:</strong> Developing critical thinking skills
to evaluate online sources for credibility and bias, distinguishing between
reliable and unreliable information, understanding how to conduct effective
research using appropriate academic databases and resources, and recognising
the importance of fact-checking and verification.</p>
<h3><strong>9.3 Respectful Online Communication</strong></h3>
<p><strong>Professional Communication Standards:</strong> All digital communications
should reflect the respect and courtesy expected in face-to-face interactions,
using appropriate language and tone in emails, discussion forums, and collaborative
platforms, maintaining constructive dialogue even when disagreeing with
others, and representing Loyang View Secondary School positively in all
online contexts.</p>
<p><strong>Cultural Sensitivity:</strong> Recognising and respecting diverse
perspectives and backgrounds in online discussions, avoiding language or
behaviour that could be offensive or discriminatory, and contributing to
an inclusive digital learning environment where all students feel valued
and respected.</p>
<h3><strong>9.4 Digital Footprint Awareness</strong></h3>
<p><strong>Long-term Impact:</strong> Understanding that digital actions create
permanent records that may affect future academic and career opportunities,
making thoughtful decisions about what to share, post, or communicate online,
and considering how digital behaviour reflects personal character and school
values.</p>
<p><strong>Personal Branding:</strong> Developing awareness of how online
presence contributes to personal reputation, maintaining consistency between
online and offline behaviour, and using technology to showcase positive
achievements and contributions to the learning community.</p>
<h3><strong>9.5 Empathy and Support in Digital Spaces</strong></h3>
<p>Students are encouraged to support their peers in digital learning environments,
report concerning behaviour to trusted adults when appropriate, offer help
to classmates struggling with technology, and contribute to a positive,
collaborative online learning culture that reflects our school's caring
community values.</p>
<h2><strong>10. Parental and Guardian Involvement</strong></h2>
<h3><strong>10.1 Shared Responsibility</strong></h3>
<p>The successful implementation of this Acceptable Use Policy requires collaboration
between school, students, and families. Parents and guardians play a crucial
role in reinforcing digital citizenship values and supporting responsible
technology use both at school and at home.</p>
<h3><strong>10.2 Home Use Guidelines</strong></h3>
<p><strong>Consistent Expectations:</strong> We encourage families to establish
home technology rules that align with school expectations, supporting the
development of healthy digital habits that extend beyond school hours and
reinforcing the values of integrity, respect, responsibility, resilience,
and compassion in all digital interactions.</p>
<p><strong>Communication and Support:</strong> Parents are encouraged to maintain
open dialogue with their children about online experiences, monitor home
use of school devices appropriately, and contact the school if they have
concerns about their child's digital behaviour or if they need support
in addressing technology-related issues.</p>
<h3><strong>10.3 Incident Communication</strong></h3>
<p>Parents will be notified promptly of any significant policy violations
or concerning digital behaviour, and the school welcomes parental input
in developing appropriate responses to technology-related incidents that
support student learning and growth.</p>
<h2><strong>11. Policy Review and Updates</strong></h2>
<h3><strong>11.1 Regular Review Process</strong></h3>
<p>This Acceptable Use Policy will be reviewed annually to ensure it remains
current with technological developments, educational best practices, and
regulatory requirements. Input from students, parents, teachers, and staff
will be considered during review processes.</p>
<h3><strong>11.2 Communication of Changes</strong></h3>
<p>Any updates to this policy will be communicated to students and parents
through official school channels, including the school website, parent
portals, and direct communication as appropriate. Students will receive
updated guidance and training when significant changes are implemented.</p>
<h2><strong>12. Acknowledgement and Agreement</strong></h2>
<p>By using Loyang View Secondary School's educational technology resources,
students acknowledge that they have read, understood, and agree to comply
with this Acceptable Use Policy. Students understand that violation of
this policy may result in disciplinary action and loss of technology privileges.</p>
<p>This policy supports our school's mission to create a vibrant and caring
environment where students are nurtured to strive towards their full potential,
empowering every individual to SHINE as responsible digital citizens and
future-ready leaders.</p>
