<h2><strong>1. Purpose and Introduction</strong></h2>
<p>This Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) establishes guidelines for the responsible
and effective use of Personal Learning Devices (PLDs) and educational technology
resources at Loyang View Secondary School. Aligned with our purpose of
empowering every individual to SHINE, this policy creates a safe, productive
digital learning environment that enhances educational outcomes whilst
protecting the privacy, safety, and wellbeing of all members of our school
community.</p>
<p>Technology is an essential part of learning in today's world. As we nurture
our students to become lifelong learners, future-ready leaders, and active
citizens, this AUP helps you understand how to use EdTech resources safely
and responsibly, in line with efforts to develop your digital literacies
and growth as digitally responsible citizens.</p>
<p>Through responsible use of technology, we cultivate our school culture
of Learn, Value, Shine - where students learn effectively through digital
tools, value respectful online behaviour, and shine as responsible digital
citizens in our vibrant and caring school community.</p>
<p>For additional resources, please refer to: Student Kit on Cyber Wellness
and Learning with a Personal Learning Device (Secondary) – <a href="https://go.gov.sg/cw-studentkit" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank"><u>https://go.gov.sg/cw-studentkit</u></a>
</p>
<h2><strong>2. Scope and Application</strong></h2>
<p>This policy applies to all students, teachers, staff, and visitors using
school technology resources, including but not limited to:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Personal Learning Devices (iPads) provided by or used within the school</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>ICT system accounts (e.g. Singapore Student Learning Space (SLS), iCON,
Google Classroom, Microsoft 365)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>School internet networks</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>All educational technology platforms and applications accessible through
school systems</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Use of school technology resources both on and off campus during school-related
activities</p>
</li>
</ul>
<h2><strong>3. General Guidelines</strong></h2>
<p>You should not attempt to access data, systems and information that you
are not authorised to access. You are reminded that the use of learning
devices and school's EdTech resources should solely be for the purpose
of learning.</p>
<h2><strong>4. Core Requirements and Technical Standards</strong></h2>
<h3><strong>4.1 Device Management Application (DMA) Requirement</strong></h3>
<p><strong>All Personal Learning Devices must have the Device Management Application (DMA) installed and active before they can be used in school for lessons.</strong> All
PLDs are installed with a DMA to support a safe and conducive learning
environment. You should not attempt to bypass the DMA.</p>
<p>Devices without DMA will be treated according to existing school smartphone
policies, including immediate confiscation and storage in the Student Welfare
room. Further consequences may apply, up to and including withholding devices
until parents collect them personally.</p>
<h3><strong>4.2 Using Your PLD - Responsibilities and Care</strong></h3>
<p><strong>Device Responsibility:</strong> You are responsible for your PLD.
In the event of loss or theft of the device, you must make a police report
and report the matter to the school immediately.</p>
<p><strong>Charging and Maintenance:</strong> In order to have a positive
learning experience, you should ensure that your PLD is fully charged before
bringing it to school. Should you need to charge your PLD in school, you
should only do so at the school's designated charging stations, during
recess and/or after-school hours.</p>
<p><strong>System Updates:</strong> You are reminded to keep your PLD updated
by installing the relevant patches and updates when being prompted by the
system.</p>
<p><strong>Data Backup:</strong> You are responsible for regularly backing
up your learning materials in your PLD to prevent data loss using the 200GB
complimentary iCloud storage provided through your school-managed Apple
ID.</p>
<h3><strong>4.3 Apple ID and Account Management</strong></h3>
<p>Students must use only school-managed Apple IDs provided by Loyang View
Secondary School. Personal Apple IDs are not permitted on school devices
to ensure security, appropriate content access, and compliance with educational
data protection requirements.</p>
<h3><strong>4.4 Application Installation and Management</strong></h3>
<p>All applications must be installed through the JAMF Student app using
the school's approved whitelist. You may only download applications that
appear in your JAMF Student app catalogue that have been vetted and approved
by the school.</p>
<h3><strong>4.5 Camera and Recording Policy</strong></h3>
<p>The camera and recording functions on PLDs may only be used with explicit
approval from the subject teacher to protect student and staff privacy
rights and ensure compliance with data protection regulations.</p>
<h2><strong>5. Being a Responsible Digital Citizen</strong></h2>
<p>As members of the Loyang View Secondary School community, students are
expected to demonstrate our school values of Integrity, Respect, Responsibility,
Resilience, and Compassion in all digital interactions:</p>
<p><strong>Respectful Communication:</strong> You should interact with others
in a respectful and responsible way. You should not post online remarks
that are racially and/or religiously insensitive, vulgar and/or offensive,
or hurtful to others.</p>
<p><strong>Appropriate Content:</strong> You should not use any devices to
store, modify, create or share content (e.g. documents, presentations,
pictures and videos) that is inappropriate (e.g. indecent, pornographic,
defamatory, hurtful to self or others), or make threats, cause harassment
or embarrassment, impersonate or intimidate others.</p>
<p><strong>Educational Purpose:</strong> You should not use MOE/school-deployed
ICT system accounts for any commercial activities (e.g. buying and selling
of goods and services).</p>
<p><strong>Data and Privacy Protection:</strong> Students are expected to
protect both their own and others' personal information by maintaining
confidentiality of login credentials, respecting privacy settings on school
platforms, and reporting any suspected security breaches immediately.</p>
<h2><strong>6. Respecting Copyright</strong></h2>
<p><strong>Permission and Attribution:</strong> You are reminded to obtain
explicit permission from the content owner(s) before downloading, copying
or sharing any copyrighted materials (e.g. pictures, videos, music). You
must properly cite sources and references in all academic work.</p>
<p><strong>Legal Compliance:</strong> You should not use, copy, edit or share
digital files in an unauthorised or illegal manner. This includes avoiding
plagiarism and respecting intellectual property rights of content creators.</p>
<h2><strong>7. Using Artificial Intelligence (AI)</strong></h2>
<p><strong>Age Requirements:</strong> You must ensure that you meet the minimum
age requirement specified in each AI tool's age restrictions before using
it.</p>
<p><strong>Academic Integrity:</strong> If the use of AI is permitted, you
should acknowledge the use of AI in weighted assessments and homework as
required. You are to practice academic integrity and be responsible for
your own learning when using AI. Understand that you are ultimately short-changing
yourself if you pass off other's work as your own.</p>
<p><strong>Critical Evaluation:</strong> You should exercise caution regarding
the limitations of AI tools, including potential inaccuracies/fabricated
responses, inherent biases and outdated information.</p>
<h2><strong>8. Staying Safe and Secure Online</strong></h2>
<p><strong>Incident Reporting:</strong> You should report any incidents (e.g.
unusual device behaviour or inappropriate use of devices) to your Form
Teacher or the school ICT team immediately.</p>
<p><strong>Online Safety Habits:</strong> You are reminded to develop online
safety habits, including:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Not disclosing personal access credentials (e.g. MIMS password, PLD passcode,
etc.) or sensitive personal data (e.g. home address, passwords) online
or on AI platforms</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Verifying credibility of online content before sharing</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Avoiding clicking on suspicious links or downloading unknown files</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Being cautious when interacting with others online (e.g. on social media)
by not engaging with strangers</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Information Literacy:</strong> Develop critical thinking skills
to evaluate online sources for credibility and bias, and recognise the
importance of fact-checking and verification.</p>
<h2><strong>9. Digital Wellbeing and Balance</strong></h2>
<p><strong>Balanced Technology Use:</strong> You are reminded to balance screentime
with other activities including physical exercise and face-to-face social
interactions. You should avoid excessive use of your devices outside learning
hours.</p>
<p><strong>Health Considerations:</strong> You should take regular breaks
to rest your eyes and mind during extended technology use.</p>
<p><strong>Sleep Hygiene:</strong> You are reminded to practice good sleep
hygiene by not using your devices one hour before bedtime.</p>
<h2><strong>10. Prohibited Activities</strong></h2>
<p>The following activities are strictly forbidden:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Bypassing security measures, including content filters, firewalls, or
the DMA system</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Accessing inappropriate material that is violent, sexually explicit, discriminatory,
or illegal</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Engaging in cyberbullying, harassment, or threatening behaviour</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Unauthorised access to other users' accounts or personal information</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Commercial activities or profit-making activities unrelated to education</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Unauthorised recording without proper teacher approval</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Identity misrepresentation or creating fake accounts</p>
</li>
</ul>
<h2><strong>11. Consequences and Enforcement</strong></h2>
<p>Non-compliance with this AUP will lead to disciplinary action in accordance
with the school's discipline policy. The school employs a progressive discipline
approach that emphasises learning from mistakes while maintaining clear
boundaries, with consequences ranging from warnings and temporary restrictions
to extended suspension of technology privileges and formal disciplinary
action.</p>
<h2><strong>12. Monitoring and Privacy Considerations</strong></h2>
<p>The school maintains appropriate monitoring systems to ensure student
safety, network security, and policy compliance whilst respecting student
privacy rights. All student data is protected according to established
data protection protocols and accessed only by authorised personnel.</p>
<h2><strong>13. Parental and Guardian Involvement</strong></h2>
<p>The successful implementation of this policy requires collaboration between
school, students, and families. Parents are encouraged to establish home
technology rules that align with school expectations and maintain open
dialogue about online experiences.</p>
<h2><strong>14. Policy Review and Updates</strong></h2>
<p>This policy will be reviewed annually to ensure it remains current with
technological developments and educational best practices. Updates will
be communicated through official school channels.</p>
<h2><strong>15. Acknowledgement and Agreement</strong></h2>
<p>By using Loyang View Secondary School's educational technology resources,
students acknowledge that they have read, understood, and agree to comply
with this Acceptable Use Policy. This policy supports our school's mission
to create a vibrant and caring environment where students are nurtured
to strive towards their full potential, empowering every individual to
SHINE as responsible digital citizens and future-ready leaders.</p>
